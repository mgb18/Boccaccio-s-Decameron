Pampinea

Pampinea is the eldest of all the story tellers. In many ways she becomes the leader of The Decameron, and throughout the stories proves herself to be one of the most mature and balanced members of the group. Pampinea opens The Decameron's first day, and does not prescribe any set theme. In addition, she narrates the Third Story in Day Two, the Second Story in Day Three, the Second Story in Day Four, the Sixth Story in Day Five, the Second Story in Day Six, the Sixth Story in Day Seven, the Seventh Story in Day Eight, The Nineth Story in Day Seven, and the Tenth Story in Day Seven.
