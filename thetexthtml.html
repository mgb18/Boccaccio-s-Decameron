The History and Setting of the Text

The Decameron was written in fourteenth century by Italian author Giovanni Boccaccio. It is considered to be a classic work of early Italian literature. The Decameron takes place during the time in which the black death ravaged the city of Florence. Each of the stories is told in a fictional villa outside of Florence. The characters gather in a garden outside the villa, and tell each other stories that take place in a multitidue of locals, cities to hidden castles. Each story is told by one particular story teller. Each day has a set theme dictated by one of the characters. However, it is important to note that the Decameron is known for its raunchy tales filled with sexual exploits people don't typically associate with the such an ancient text. It is the humorous and at times explicit stories that The Decameron is known for.
